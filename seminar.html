OUR SEMINAR

1 Modern Life and the Mysterious Krishna

In the chaos of modern life filled with stress, competition, and distraction, Lord Krishna emerges as a timeless guide. His teachings from the Bhagavad Gita offer clarity in confusion, calm in chaos, and direction in dilemmas. Krishna symbolizes action with detachment, love with wisdom, and leadership with humility. By understanding Krishna’s message, today’s youth can develop emotional resilience, moral strength, and inner peace.

2 Shiv-Sanskars: Past, Present, and Future

Shiv-Sanskars are the noble values and ideals inspired by the life of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. These include fearless leadership, justice for all, honor for women, respect for all religions, and a vision for Swarajya rooted in ethics. In a time when youth are searching for identity and purpose, Shivaji’s life offers a living example of how to lead with courage and integrity. Shiv-Sanskars are not relics of the past—they are active principles that can shape the present and inspire the future. Whether in governance, education, or social work, these values remain relevant.

3 Family Meetings and Relationships

In the age of digital screens and individual routines, emotional bonding within families is weakening. Misunderstandings, communication gaps, and generational conflicts are growing. The concept of Family Meetings offers a practical solution to rebuild trust, understanding, and emotional closeness. A weekly family meeting creates a safe space for all members—from children to elders—to express thoughts, resolve conflicts, plan together, and share achievements. This practice strengthens emotional intelligence, respect, and unity within the home.

4 Rising Expectations of Youth and Marriage Challenges

Today's youth are more aware, ambitious, and self-driven than ever before. They dream big—personally and professionally. However, this has also led to increased emotional stress, unrealistic expectations, fear of commitment, and delayed or failed marriages. Social media and changing cultural norms add more confusion.Understanding their aspirations and providing non-judgmental, value-based guidance is essential. Parents, educators, and counselors must work together to help youth navigate relationships, career pressures, and marriage decisions with balance and maturity.

5 The Illusion of Advertising and Your Role as a Victim

Advertisements are no longer just informative—they are persuasive, manipulative, and emotionally targeted. They shape our desires, redefine our needs, and influence our identity, especially among youth and children. From beauty products to lifestyle brands, the message is: you are incomplete without this product. We must build awareness to question these illusions. Media literacy, critical thinking, and value-based education are essential to free ourselves from this consumerist trap.

6 Career Counseling for Students

In a world of endless options and constant competition, students often face confusion, pressure, and anxiety about choosing the right career. Many follow trends, parental expectations, or peer choices without understanding their own strengths, interests, and values. Effective career counseling helps students explore themselves first—what they are good at, what they love doing, and where they can contribute meaningfully. It includes aptitude testing, personality analysis, and exposure to diverse fields—science, arts, commerce, vocational and emerging careers.
